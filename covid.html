Covid 19 data exploration

üéØ Goal and Research Questions

The goal of this project was to clean, transform, and analyze COVID-19 data to understand infection, death, and vaccination trends globally and for specific countries. The main research questions were:

  • Which countries and continents experienced the highest infection and death rates?
  • What percentage of the population has been vaccinated over time?
  • How do new cases and deaths correlate with vaccination progress?
  • What trends can be observed at global, continental, and national levels?

üìä Data Sources

The data was sourced from Our World in Data ‚Äì COVID-19 dataset and includes:

  • CovidDeaths: Daily counts of COVID-19 cases, deaths, and population per location
  • CovidVaccinations: Daily vaccination counts per location
  • Both tables were stored in Microsoft SQL Server under the PortfolioProject.dbo schema

üîÑ Process (Cleaning, Transformation, Exploration)

  • Selected relevant columns for analysis, including location, continent, date, total and new cases/deaths, and population
  • Calculated death percentages for specific countries (e.g., Poland) using total_deaths / total_cases * 100
  • Computed infection rates relative to population for countries and identified countries with the highest infection rates
  • Cleaned data issues, such as rows where continent names were incorrectly added to the location column
  • Aggregated total deaths by continent and by country
  • Joined deaths and vaccinations tables to analyze vaccination progress over time
  • Calculated rolling totals of vaccinated individuals per country using SUM() OVER (PARTITION BY location ORDER BY date)
  • Computed percentage of population vaccinated based on rolling totals
  • Created a view PercentPopulationVaccinatedView for easier future querying and visualization
  • Performed exploratory analysis on global, continental, and national trends in cases, deaths, and vaccinations

üìà Results and Findings

  • Identified countries with the highest infection rates relative to population
  • Determined countries and continents with the highest total deaths
  • Tracked vaccination progress, including cumulative counts and percentage of population vaccinated over time
  • Observed trends linking vaccination progress with new cases and deaths at global and national levels
  • Created clean, transformed, and query-ready datasets and views to support further analysis and visualizations

‚ö†Ô∏è Limitations and Future Work

  • Data quality issues, including missing continent information and mislabelled location names, required cleaning
  • Analysis is limited to reported data; underreporting or delays in reporting may affect accuracy
  • Vaccination data does not include information on vaccine type or effectiveness
  • Future work could include combining with mobility or testing data for more detailed modeling
  • Advanced time series or predictive models could be applied to forecast cases, deaths, or vaccination outcomes
