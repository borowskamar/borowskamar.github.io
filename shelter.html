Analysis of dog adoptions with Python

üéØGoal and Research Questions

The goal of this project was to understand the factors influencing the length of stay of dogs in a shelter and identify patterns in dog adoptions. Key research questions include:
  • How long do dogs stay in the shelter before being adopted?
  • Are there differences in stay duration based on breed, age, gender, or weight?
  • Which dog characteristics are associated with faster adoption?
  • Do dogs with black fur wait longer for adoption?

üìä Data Sources

The dataset comes from a Warsaw animal shelter and contains information about:

  • Intake and adoption dates
  • Breed, age, gender and weight of the dogs
  • Fur color (black/not black)
  • Return status (returned/not returned)
    • Data was provided in CSV format and loaded into Python for analysis.

üîÑ Process (ETL, Transformations, Exploration, Modeling)

üîß ETL and Transformations

  • Loaded data from CSV
  • Removed duplicates, missing values and illogical values
  • Converted data types (e.g., dates to datetime)
  • Calculated the length of stay in the shelter for each dog
  • Handled outliers

üîç Exploratory Data Analysis

  • Identified correlations between dog characteristics and adoption speed
  • Investigated trends in adoptions (e.g., by month)
  • Analyzed adoption return rates
  • Analyzed distributions of days spent in the shelter, weight, age, gender and breed

ü§ñ Modeling

  • Started with Linear Regression to test the impact of age and weight on shelter stay, then expanded the model to include gender, fur color, breed status, and return history
  • Created interaction features (e.g., age √ó weight, male √ó black fur, small & young dogs) to capture combined effects
  • Applied Random Forest and XGBoost to capture non-linear relationships and interactions. Hyperparameter tuning was used to improve predictive performance
  • Evaluated all models using Mean Absolute Error (MAE) and R¬≤

üìà Results and Findings

  • Age and weight are the strongest predictors: older and heavier dogs tend to stay longer
  • Physical features (sex, fur color, purebred status) have smaller but noticeable effects
  • Interaction terms improved model accuracy, highlighting that combinations of traits influence adoption speed
  • XGBoost achieved the best performance after tuning: R¬≤ = 0.34, MAE ‚âà 42 days, showing that these factors explain about one-third of the variation in shelter stay duration
  • Seasonal factors and adoption year/month had minimal predictive value

Limitations:

  • The dataset is limited to a single shelter, which may reduce generalizability
  • Key factors such as behavioral traits, health status, and promotional efforts were not available
  • Models explain only a portion of the variance (R¬≤ ‚âà 0.34 for the best model), indicating that many other factors influence shelter stay

Future Work:

  • Expand the analysis to multiple shelters for a more representative understanding
  • Include additional features such as temperament, medical history, and adoption campaigns
  • Experiment with advanced models and ensemble techniques, including deep learning, to improve predictive accuracy
  • Investigate seasonal and local community effects more deeply to guide shelter strategies
